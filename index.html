Protocol Steps

  1. Alice chooses random bits and random bases: rectilinear + or diagonal ×.
  2. Alice encodes each bit into a photon polarization state.
  3. Eve may intercept some photons, measure them in a random basis, and resend them.
  4. Bob measures each photon in a random basis of his own.
  5. Alice and Bob publicly compare bases, keeping only positions where their bases matched.
  6. They sacrifice some kept bits to estimate the quantum bit error rate. The rest become the secret key.

Why BB84 Matters

  • It shows how quantum measurement disturbs information.
  • If Eve measures in the wrong basis, she introduces detectable errors.
  • Alice and Bob do not need to reveal the final key itself, only some test bits.
  • The error rate, or QBER, tells them whether the channel looks safe.
